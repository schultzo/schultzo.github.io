Bootcamp Learning Plan

What is your long term goal and/or career pathway?

My long term goal is to be able to make a positive difference on the world through the use of tech. I like the idea of projects like vaxx.nz and Time in the line where a technical solution can be made to improve a problem in the world. I'm still unsure around whether I want a full time developer role where I don't get to decide what to develop. I like the creative aspect of design so I think I would find it hard if that was taken away and was someone elses role to do.

Strengths:

  • I can recognise when I am getting frustrated with something and need a break from it and can identify what is a worthwhile less frustrating task to do in the mean time.
  • When I get stuck on something I don't give up and will try to come up with a solution
  • I am organised and when struggling with something will attempt to become more structured with my learning by generally listing out what I am stuck with and breaking it out into more managable chunks.

Weaknesses

  • I like to understand the why and am more engaged in trying to figure out the problem if I want to achieve the end result
  • I doubt my ability to achieve some tasks - sometimes challenges can feel too technical and out of my depth but then I surprise myself when I manage to figure it out
  • I find it difficult to visualise something just in my head and so I find it difficult to picture what it will look like until I see it with my eyes. Also, with logic I feel like my brain sometimes makes something simple, super complicated.

What skills (non-technical - human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

Technical skills

I would like to be able to leave Dev Academy with enough knowledge to be able come up with a solution to a problem if I identify it. I don't expect to know everything but with enough knowledge that I would be able to figure it out for myself. I would really like to be able to be comfortable with using APIs from different sites so I can "play" with them and try doing cool things with them!

Non Technical skills

I would like to learn how to have more fun/be less serious while still achieving high results. I would also like to feel more comfortable reaching out to new people and working with them.

A commitment to how you will manage your workload in this programme. This should include: An explanation of how you will manage yourself to work productively and safely with other learners, facilitators and industry/community representatives

I am going into bootcamp with the expectation that the courseload will be quite intensive and as such am expecting to dedicate majority of my time to this course. I may need to work a little throughout but am limited to a maxiumum of 10hours a week.

What you will do when things are building up and starting to get to you

I will try to be mindful of the rising stress levels and attempt to put it into perspective before it becomes too overwhelming. If I find myself stuck I will work on identifying what it is that is making me stressed and break it down to find a way forward. I will communicate how I am going to the facilitators and reach out for support if I find I am needing it. I will also use exercise as a way to step a way from the problem and give my mind a break as to come back to the issue feeling more fresh

A commitment as to how and where you will seek help in a timely way

If there is something I am stuck on I will first try and resolve by myself by trying to isolate the problem and use online resources to troubleshoot what's going on. If I cannot resolve by myself I will reach out and ask another student for assistance. If still stuck I will seek the guidance from a facilitator.

A description of what you expect from the facilitation team

I would like to ensure what I am learning is Industry best practice and that if I am going off course in anyway they are able to advise me of this

Any scheduling information such as block-out times when you are committed to other things. For example, you might have whānau commitments like school drop-offs. It’s important to plan these in.

I don't have any other commitments that I can forsee blocking me in some way - The course is my number one priority until it finishes
