Neuroplasticity

Neuroplasticity is an umbrela term for the brains ability to change over time and throughout the individuals lifetime. It allows for the brain to form new connections or allow for pathways to be reformed after they become broken. It creates new paths from what is already there - neurogenesis is the brains abiltiy to create new neurons.

Fun Fact: The term “neuroplasticity” was first used by Polish neuroscientist Jerzy Konorski in 1948

Discuss how understanding the principles of neuroplasticity benefits people

The concept of neuroplasticity can benefit people in multiple ways, at its core the proof of neuroplasticity cant give hope to individuals who may have certain struggles that things have the potential to improve. The brain has the ability to "rewire" itself and forge new paths when other ones may have been broken from an illness or impact to the brain. It enables people to grow and recover.

Discuss how you might engage with the principles of neuroplasticity for your own benefit

Personally, I have always disliked the saying "you can't teach an old dog new tricks", I've always been of the belief that individuals can do whatever they want, possibly if you get older it gets harder as a certain way of being has become a more foundational part of your life but I do believe anyone can change if they really want to. For me I will be aware of thoughts/beliefs that I cannot change and try to recognise whether its a belief or fact.

What is a growth mindset

Growth and fixed mindset is two different ways of looking at your approach to learning and life in general. A fixed mindset is one where you believe your current way of being is the way you are and that it wont change. It comes along with saying things like "I can't do that" "That's not how I do things" , "I like things this way". The alternative is a growth mindset whereby you are more open to the idea of learning, trying new things and that your current being is not the extent of all you can be.

For me I have been familar with growth vs fixed mindset before and I do consider myself for the most part to have a growth mindset. There are certain areas which I do thing I'm more fixed in my beliefs but I think the first step is recognising that and working on trying to change that. I think throughout my learning journey with Dev Academy I will need to continue to be mindful of when I may be experiencing a fixed mindset and working on actively changing that.
